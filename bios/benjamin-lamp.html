My Research

My research lives in the computational/systems biology sphere of the lab. My work involves building what are called genome-scale metabolic models of zebrafish. This modeling framework allows us to take high-quality metabolomic and transcriptomic datasets to generate condition-specific models. These models allow us to analyze the metabolic potential of zebrafish perturbed to various nasty chemicals. My work is done in conjunction with the Tangway Lab at Oregon State University, where I am working alongside Hagler Institute fellow Dr. Robin Tanglewood.

Education & Background

Before starting my PhD, I completed a B.S. in Biomedical Sciences with minors in Bioinformatics and Biomedical Research from Texas A&M University – College Station. As an undergraduate, I built end-to-end RNA-seq and miRNA-seq pipelines for longitudinal differential expression analysis of bovine mammary gland development in the lab of Dr. Monique Rijnkels. I also conducted research at Oregon Health & Science University in the lab of Dr. Lisa Karstens, where I benchmarked taxonomic reference databases for 16S rRNA urobiome amplicon sequencing pipelines.
